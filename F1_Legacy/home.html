How Formula 1 Works

The Championships

There are two primary championships in Formula 1:

  • The World Driver Championship (WDC): This is won by the driver who accumulates the highest number of points throughout the season.
  • The World Constructor Championship (WCC): This is won by the team that scores the most points, which is the combined total of points earned by both drivers for that team.

The Points System

Points are awarded to the top 10 finishers in a Grand Prix race:

  • P1 (Place 1): 25 Points
  • P2: 18 Points
  • P3: 15 Points
  • P4: 12 Points
  • P5: 10 Points
  • P6: 8 Points
  • P7: 6 Points
  • P8: 4 Points
  • P9: 2 Points
  • P10: 1 Point

Drivers finishing below 10th place do not earn any points.

What a Normal Weekend Looks Like

  • Thursday: Media Day (interviews, press conferences)
  • Friday: FP1 and FP2 (Free Practice sessions) for car setup and track familiarization.
  • Saturday: FP3 (final Free Practice) and Qualifying. Qualifying determines the starting grid order for Sunday's race.
  • Sunday: Race Day â€“ The main event where drivers compete for points!

What a Sprint Weekend Looks Like

A Sprint Weekend introduces an additional short race:

  • Thursday: Same as normal weekend (Media Day).
  • Friday: FP1 and Sprint Qualifying (determines grid for the Sprint Race).
  • Saturday: The Sprint Race (a shorter race that awards points) and Qualifying (determines grid for the main Grand Prix).
  • Sunday: Race Day.
